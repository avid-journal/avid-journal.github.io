01 · The problem

Why this matters

In February 2026, Axiom Math presented the Fel conjecture as a new contribution to AI-assisted mathematics. It turned out to be a misrepresented literature search result.1 Current AI systems for mathematics measure correctness — they do not measure novelty. AViD Journal closes that gap.

1The episode that motivated AViD: a result presented as new that an adequate prior-work search would have flagged — exactly the failure mode novelty assessment is meant to catch.
Current systems
  • Verify that a proof type-checks
  • Optimize for correctness and pass-rate
  • Blind to prior formal and informal work
  • Cannot separate automation from discovery
AViD Journal
  • Assumes correctness, measures novelty
  • Searches formal libraries and literature
  • Scores triviality against today's tactics
  • Returns a verdict with full evidence
A result that type-checks is not the same as a result that is new. The distance between those two statements is exactly where misrepresentation lives.
02 · The method

Three dimensions of novelty

Each theorem submitted to AViD is evaluated across three independent dimensions, applied in sequence.

D1Existence

Does this theorem already exist in formal libraries (mathlib) or in informal literature (arXiv)? We query Leandex for formal matches and Semantic Scholar for informal prior work.

D2Triviality

Can the statement be closed by standard tactics — decide, omega, simp, norm_num, aesop, tauto, exact? — within a fixed budget? Triviality is relative to the current state of mathlib automation. A result trivial today may not have been trivial a year ago.2

2Triviality is not a property of a statement alone, but of a statement paired with an automation frontier. Every D2 verdict is implicitly stamped with the mathlib revision it ran against.
D3Proof distance

When the statement exists but proofs differ, we measure Jaccard distance over the set of premises extracted from the elaborated Lean 4 proof term — a proof-object-level signal, not a syntactic one.3

3Operating on the proof object means two proofs that read very differently in source but invoke the same lemmas register as close — and a genuinely different argument registers as far.

04 · Open research

What we're investigating

AViD is a working system, but the underlying questions remain open. These are the directions we're actively exploring.

Q.01

Proof representation

How to construct proof representations that are robust to formalization variation and capture the mathematical distinction between alternative proofs, generalization, specialization, and statement-level novelty?

Q.02

Generalization vs novelty

How to distinguish formally between "generalization of A" and "novel result related to A" — beyond what current LLM judges can reliably do?

Q.03

Autoformalization fidelity

How to measure semantic fidelity of autoformalization? Statements that type-check but misrepresent the original paper's claim are a critical bottleneck — and currently undetectable without human review.

Q.04

Impact measurement

How to quantify the impact of a mathematical contribution beyond binary novelty? Can we measure whether a result opens new directions, connects previously separate areas, or enables proofs that were previously out of reach?
